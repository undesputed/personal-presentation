Q&A Cheat Sheet

想定される質問と、答えのヒント

Likely questions from the PHD team, with simple answers built from your real story. Each question is also written in Japanese so you know what they mean, even if the English is unclear. These are drafts — say them in your own words.

🗣️ Before you answer — 5 quick tips

  • 1.Speak slowly and simply. Short sentences. Their English is rusty. ゆっくり、短く話す。
  • 2.Point at a slide. Show, don't only tell. スライドを指さして見せる。
  • 3.Give one real example for each answer — a story is easy to follow. 具体例を1つ話す。
  • 4.If unsure what they asked, say: "May I repeat the question to check?" 質問を確認してOK。
  • 5.It's okay to pause and think. Silence is fine. 少し考えてから答えて大丈夫。
🌄 About your journey
1Why did you choose IT / software development?
なぜ IT・ソフトウェア開発の道を選びましたか？
Say something like
  • "In the Philippines, education decides your future. I chose IT because it is a skill I can grow forever, and it can lift my family."
  • Mention you liked building things that people actually use.
2Was it hard to study and work at the same time?
学業と仕事の両立は大変でしたか？
Say something like
  • "Yes, it was tiring. But I chose it. I wanted experience before graduation, so companies would say yes to me."
  • This is your proof of initiative and diskarte — say it with pride.
3What was the hardest moment, and how did you get through it?
一番大変だった時期と、その乗り越え方は？
Say something like
  • Pick one real story (a hard project, a tight deadline, learning a new language like Java or Laravel fast).
  • "It was hard, so I broke the problem into small steps, studied at night, and asked for help. I finished it."
💡 Diskarte & problem-solving
4Can you give an example of "diskarte" — finding a creative way?
「ディスカルテ」＝工夫した具体例を教えてください。
Say something like
  • Working while still a student to fill the experience gap is your best example.
  • Or: a time you found a simpler, faster solution on a project when the normal way was blocked.
5How do you handle a difficult project or a tight deadline?
難しい案件や、厳しい納期にどう対応しますか？
Say something like
  • "First I plan and split the work. I talk early with the client and the team. I focus on the most important parts first."
🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Leadership
6How did you become a team leader? What changed for you?
どうやってリーダーになりましたか？何が変わりましたか？
Say something like
  • "I grew step by step. Before, I only wrote code. Now I also plan, guide the team, and talk with clients."
  • Show slide 10 (Developer → Leader).
7What is your leadership style? How do you guide your team?
あなたのリーダーシップのスタイルは？
Say something like
  • "I listen first. I explain the goal clearly, share tasks by strength, and support them when they are stuck."
🇯🇵 Working with Japan
8How do you communicate with Japanese clients?
日本のお客様と、どのように意思疎通していますか？
Say something like
  • "I keep it simple and confirm everything. I use documents, screenshots, and diagrams so we understand each other clearly."
  • Optional: mention any Japanese words/phrases you are learning. 日本語を勉強中、と伝えても好印象。
9What do you like about working with a Japanese company?
日本の会社で働く良さは何ですか？
Say something like
  • "I respect the quality, the care for detail, and clear teamwork. I want to grow with that culture."
🚀 Future & you
10What are you most proud of?
一番誇りに思うことは何ですか？
Say something like
  • "That I started from zero and became a team leader. And that my clients trust the systems my team builds."
11Where do you want to be in 3–5 years? What is your next goal?
3〜5年後の目標は？次に挑戦したいことは？
Say something like
  • "I want to lead bigger projects, grow more engineers, and go deeper into AI." （AI に興味あり）
12What are your strengths and weaknesses?
あなたの強みと弱みは？
Say something like
  • Strength: "I learn fast and I never give up — diskarte."
  • Weakness: pick a real one + how you are improving it (e.g. "I want to say no more often, so I am learning to protect the team's time.")

If they ask: "What is diskarte?"

Diskarte is a Filipino word. It means resourcefulness — finding your own way to solve a problem, even with few resources. Not waiting. Taking action.

「ディスカルテ」＝ 少ない resources でも、自分で工夫して道を切りひらく力。待たずに動くこと。
